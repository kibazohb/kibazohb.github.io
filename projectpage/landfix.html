LAND_FIX

N.S.B.E HACKS @UoFT

Background

N.S.B.E Hacks was a hackathon hosted by UoFT's National Society of Black Engineers' Chapter. This was Canada's first ever hackathon to be hosted by a N.S.B.E chapter. The main speaker inspired the audience to come up with ideas that would be of significance in solving problems that people in the black community faced all over the world.

Our approach

My team and I decided to work on Africa's corruptive land ownership management system. The main idea was to leverage IBM's blockchain and Google maps API to secure a digital record of all transactions, hence preventing powerful and corrupt individuals from tampering with the data.

I was responsible for setting up the database using Google's firebase to store customer data and also assisted on implementing Google maps API to construct a good visual representation of owned land on the map.

Special thanks to my incredible team members Guy-Jacques Isombe, Tawana Mphusu, Shreyal Shah and Olayiowola Bakare, we intend to keep working on this project in the near future. Home is where the heart is, so home is where we intend to bring change.

Technologies used: Firebase, Google Maps API, Python, Flask, IBM blockchain api
